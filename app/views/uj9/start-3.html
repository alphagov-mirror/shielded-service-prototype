Get support if you’re clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus

This service has now closed.

If you’ve already got priority access to supermarket deliveries, you’ll keep it up to 21 June.

Contact your local authority if you need urgent help getting essential supplies and cannot rely on supermarket deliveries, family, friends or neighbours.

You can read general guidance on protecting yourself if you’re clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus.


Find out what support is available

Get medical healthcare support

You should continue to seek support from the NHS for your existing health conditions.

You can access many NHS services from home, including ordering repeat prescriptions or contacting a health professional.

You can also access these services through the NHS App.


Get mental health and wellbeing support

If you need urgent help with a mental health emergency, you should go to your nearest accident and emergency department.

If you’re looking for advice then call the urgent mental health helpline to get direct access to a mental health professional. This service is open to people of all ages, 24 hours a day 7 days a week.

The NHS offers practical tips on coping with mental health issues and advice on looking after a child or young person's mental health.

If you’re feeling lonely, you can access support from the Let’s Talk Loneliness organisation.

Get support at home

There are a range of services available to help support you at home.

You can sign up to the priority service register to access support from energy suppliers and network operators including advance notice of planned power cuts and priority support in an emergency. There are many other ways communication providers can support you as a vulnerable customer.

The NHS offers tips on staying productive while working from home. There’s also advice on how to support your child while at home.

Get support from your local authority

Local councils will continue to provide support and assistance through a range of services.

You should contact your local council to check what support they can offer.



Helpful advice

Work

Everyone is currently advised to work from home where possible. If you cannot work from home, you should go to work.

Your employer is required to take steps to reduce the risk of exposure to COVID-19 in the workplace and should be able to explain to you the measures they have put in place to keep you safe at work.

  • Making workplace safe - https://www.gov.uk/guidance/working-safely-during-coronavirus-covid-19
  • Protecting vulnerable people - https://www.hse.gov.uk/coronavirus/working-safely/protect-people.htm
  • Accessibility in the workplace - https://www.gov.uk/access-to-work
  • Furough scheme - https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/coronavirus-job-retention-scheme
  • Self employment - https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/self-employment-income-support-scheme-grant-extension
  • Citizen’s advice bureau - https://www.citizensadvice.org.uk/work/
  • Acas - https://www.acas.org.uk/contact

School

Clinically extremely vulnerable pupils and students should return to their school or other educational setting from 1 April 2021.

Children who live in a household with someone who is clinically extremely vulnerable are not advised to shield and should have returned to school or college on 8 March.

  • Information on rapid lateral flow tests: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rapid-lateral-flow-testing-for-households-and-bubbles-of-school-pupils-and-staff
  • Information on protective measures in place: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/what-parents-and-carers-need-to-know-about-early-years-providers-schools-and-colleges-during-the-coronavirus-covid-19-outbreak/what-parents-need-to-know-about-early-years-providers-schools-and-colleges-during-covid-19#guidance
