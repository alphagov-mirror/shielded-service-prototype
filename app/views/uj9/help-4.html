Find out what support you can get if you’re clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus

Get food and supplies delivered

Ask for priority access to supermarket deliveries if your area is in Tier 3 (Very High alert)

Request access to priority supermarket deliveries

Access to priority supermarket delivery slots can be requested if your area is in Tier 3 (Very High alert).

If your area drops to Tier 1 or 2, and you already have priority access to supermarket deliveries, you’ll keep it.

Get volunteer support

Help with food and prescription deliveries is available from NHS Volunteer Responders during the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic.

Please call 0808 196 3646 (8am to 8pm, 7 days a week).


Use a food bank

You might be able get help from a food bank. You’ll need to be referred to a food bank. If you have a referral, you’re still allowed to travel to a food bank - either for yourself or someone who’s vulnerable.

Find out if you can get help from a foodbank (Citizens Advice)


Get mental health support

Advice and information on how to look after your mental health and wellbeing during the coronavirus (COVID-19) outbreak.

Get help with your heating bill

Request a Winter Fuel Voucher if you are clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus and claiming working age benefits

Get vitamin D supplements

Ask for a free winter supply of vitamin D

During autumn and winter, everyone is advised to take vitamin D to keep their bones and muscles healthy and to support their general health.

This is particularly important if you've been indoors over the spring and summer as you may not have been getting enough vitamin D from sunlight.

The NHS is providing a service to get free daily vitamin D supplements if you're at clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus.
