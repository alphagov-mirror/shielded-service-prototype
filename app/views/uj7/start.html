Get support if you’re clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus

Use this service if you have a medical condition which means you’re classed as being clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus.

You can use this service to:

  • ask for priority access to supermarket deliveries if your area is in Tier 3 (Very High Alert)
  • update your details - for example, your address

You can register yourself, or on behalf of someone else.

If you already have priority access to supermarket deliveries, you’ll keep it.

Register or update your details

You’ll also be asked for your NHS number. You can find it on any letter the NHS has sent you or on a prescription.

After you've registered

When you've registered with the service, follow these steps to book priority supermarket deliveries.

  1. If you do not have one already, set up an online account with one or more supermarkets (participating supermarkets are Asda, Iceland, Morrisons, Ocado, Sainsbury’s, Tesco and Waitrose).
  2. Go to the supermarket’s website and book a delivery. Participating supermarkets should recognise you as a priority customer 1 to 7 days from when we confirm to them that you’re eligible for support (we’ll send you an email, text message or letter confirming when this has happened).
  3. If you’re worried about not getting a slot, set up an online account with more than one of the participating supermarkets.

If you need help using this service

Contact your local authority if you need help registering or updating your details

If you do not live in England

This service is available in England. There's separate guidance for:

  • Northern Ireland
  • Scotland
  • Wales
